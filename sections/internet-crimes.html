The Ethics of a Cybernetic Society

The Cybernetic Society: Use and Misuse

A "Cybernetic Society" is defined by the combined action of people, machines, and computers. This alliance, built on IT, has many advantages (e-commerce, research, global communication) but also disadvantages, including the rise of internet crimes.

The core of the problem lies in the values of privacy and anonymity. Computer users want to protect their identity and work, but this same anonymity allows criminals to act. This creates a range of "troublesome issues" that are ethical or legal violations.

Types of Internet Crimes

  • Violation of Privacy: The unauthorized access to or use of someone's personal data.
  • Hacking vs. Cracking: Hackers test the limits of a system, whereas Crackers (a term for malicious hackers) intend to cause system problems or steal.
  • Viruses and Worms: A virus is malicious code that needs human help to spread, while a worm can propagate on its own, for example, by emailing itself to a user's contacts.
  • Software Piracy: The illegal copying or distribution of software, a violation of intellectual property.

The Computer User and Moral Responsibility

Given the risks of internet crime, what is our ethical duty? The concept of "Moral Responsibility" in a cybernetic society is defined as "a way of living one’s life through values and virtues, representing a strong mental condition of owning other computer users’ claims to live a virtuous or good human life."

This is an Aristotelian (Virtue Ethics) approach. It argues that a safe and just internet is not just about laws; it's about the *character* of the users.

  • It requires the "cultivation of values and corresponding virtues" such as truth, courage, fairness, and compassion.
  • The goal is to develop a "collective moral awareness" and a "sense of respect for the dignity and well-being of other computer users."
  • This is especially true for the "alpha men"—the constructive, creative leaders of the cybernetic society (e.g., programmers, tech leaders, influencers)—who have a greater responsibility due to their power and influence.

Questions & Answers: Society & Internet Crimes

Test your knowledge of the key concepts from this section.

Q1: What is a "Cybernetic Society"?

  • It represents the use of Information Technology in the combined action of people, machines, and computers, based on the alliance of industry with science and technology.

Q2: What is the difference between a Virus and a Worm?

  • A Virus is a malicious program that needs human intervention to spread (e.g., you have to open an infected email attachment).
  • A Worm is a malicious program that can propagate by itself, without human intervention (e.g., by sending copies of itself to other computers automatically).

Q3: What is the "Moral Responsibility" of a computer user?

  • It is a (Virtue Ethics-based) concept of living one's life through virtues like truth, fairness, and compassion. It means having a "strong mental condition of owning other computer users’ claims to live a virtuous or good human life" and respecting their dignity and well-being.
